Project Summary

Subset of intractable epilepsy patients undergo long-term electrocorticography (ECoG) monitoring where through surgery, intracranial electrodes are placed directly on or inserted into the brain after removing their skull in order to localize and evaluate the epileptic regions of the brain. In order to know which brain region is generating the neural data, clinicians and researchers need to be able to localize the spatial arrangement of the electrodes in relation to the patient's brain. Once this 3D reconstruction of this process is completed through co-registration of the CT and MRI scans, the electrodes need to be labeled by group and numbered so that it matches the clinical recording. This numbering system is dictated by the spatial distribution of the electrodes within each group and has been done manually by a researcher through visual inspection. In this project, I developed a GUI toolbox that allows researchers to label electrode groups and use an algorithm to automatically number the electrodes. The toolbox also serves as an electrode viewer once all of the labeling and numbering has been completed, allowing researchers to save, view, and interact with the brain and placed electrodes in a 3D environment.
