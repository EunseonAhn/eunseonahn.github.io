Project Summary

LynxDx is a biotechnology diagnostics company based in Ann Arbor, MI, and is one of the largest COVID test providers in Michigan. The company administers drive-through saliva qPCR COVID tests.

The Problem

Due to the rapid ramp-up in COVID diagnostics needs, LynxDx was unable to focus on user-centered design in the development of their UI/UX for their laboratory information management system (LIMS) platform. In an effort to address these concerns, our team conducted a complete evaluation of the usability of its platform.

The Goals

The current project will specifically target evaluation of the client-facing side of the LIMS as utilized by the general public who sign-up for drive-through COVID testing. The goals were to: 1. Identify and understand the needs of the general public that seek out a COVID test within their community 2. Recommend ways to improve the user experience for the general public using LynxLIMS to register for drive-through testing, receive, print, and share the results of their COVID test.

Interviews

In order to understand the user needs when registering, administrating, and receiving results of the COVID test, our team conducted 5 user interviews with individuals who have previously taken a COVID test within the past year. We were specifically interested in gaining more insight about the following questions:

  1. What are the expectations of individuals when they sign up for a COVID test at a community testing site?
  2. What are the pain points of users signing up for, receiving, and accessing results of their COVID tests?
  3. Are some systems and companies better than others in delivering this experience? If so, which specific process was better and why?
We then used an affinity diagram to synthesize the interview data.


Affinity Diagram



Based on what we learned from the interviews, we then created this journey map to specify the process through which a user may go about interacting with the system to help identify where we may have opportunities to improve the usability through addressing user goals and pain points.



Customer Journey Map




Key Findings

1. Users want clear information about their eligibility to receive the COVID test
2. Users perceive different levels of credibility for the different methods of COVID tests
3. Users' specific priorities determine where to get tested and what test they'll receive
4. Users want clear communication about when and how they'll get their results



Survey

Expanding on the preliminary findings of our interview, with the focus around our key findings, we then developed and distributed a Qualtrics survey intended to collect quantitative data about the following overarching questions:

  • What are the user's pain points and when do they occur during their test-taking process?
  • What information do patients feel is necessary to have about their COVID-test before, during, and after their test?
  • What are the priorities for people receiving COVID tests and how do patients decide on which COVID test to receive and where?

Demographics

Our survey largely consisted of university students who had been tested at a non-LynxDx site through nasal swab. Majority of the respondents had taken 4 or more COVID tests within the past year.

Comparative Analysis

We evaluated comparable companies in the same industry sector or market niche who compete with LynxDx's products and services. For this process, we focused on comparing features about different products' ease of use and access, registration process, testing requirements, and information provided to the users. Competitors were broken into direct, indirect, partial, parallel, and analogous categories. This allowed us to compare LynxDx to a variety of companies whose product could serve as models for improvement.




With this comparative analysis, we learned:

  1. LynxDx has a more complicated registration process than its competitors. It requires more personal information than its competitors without the ability to present some of these information in-person.
  2. Other comparative products offer more streamlined process of registering and scheduling diagnostics test than LynxDx.
  3. Other comparative products offer a clear view of available appointment times prior to any registration process.
  4. Other products offer better help documentation along the registration process with obvious entrances to FAQs that offer search functionality.


Heuristic Evaluation

Through heuristic evaluation, we wanted to identify key critical usability issues present in the LynxDx registration and scheduling interface according to the established usability heuristics. We specifically adopted Jakob Nielsen’s 10 Usability Heuristics (Nielsen, 1994) to evaluate the system while adding a new heuristic we felt was applicable for our specific system. The aim of our heuristic evaluation was to address:

  • How well does the current user interface of LynxDx registration process adhere to Nielsen’s 10 Usability Heuristics?
  • What are the aspects that can be improved to meet the heuristics?
  • What aspects already are in line with the heuristics?
  • What are the ways in which we can fix the identified issues to better adhere to the standard heuristics?
The identified issues and the severity for each of these issues were combined across all evaluators and used to provide recommendations for improvements on LynxDx’s user interface.
