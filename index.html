Hi! I'm EunSeon.

I'm a Ph.D. Candidate in Cognitive Psychology and Scientific Computing at the University of Michigan, advised by Dr. David Brang.

I study multisensory perception (audio, visual, and haptic interaction/integration) and human factors using behavioral and psychophysical measures, surveys, and neuroimaging.

Using my background in research design, quantitative and qualitative data analysis, and human behavior, I am passionate about leading research that bring data driven insights about people.

Here are some projects that I've worked on:

Investigating the Neural Signal for Error Processing and Tracking

The error-related negativity (ERN) is a neural signal generated in the brain when we make mistakes and is widely used in brain-computer interface (BCI) to detect and correct for errors. In this study, we used human intracranial recordings to demonstrate that ERN can be observed during continuous performance monitoring of errors and that brain regions sensitive to errors still show error-related neural responses even when no error has occurred, likely driven by high probability of error.

Computerized Cognitive Testing Toolbox

As part of my clinical research work studying cognitive functions in tumor patients longitudinally, I designed and programmed a MATLAB based cognitive testing GUI for clinical research technicians. This toolbox allows easy interface for users who do not have any programming background to run computerized cognitive tasks, load data from different sessions, and take electronic notes on the testing session.

Role of Visual Imagery in Synesthetes

We conducted a double-blind survey and interviews to investigate the role of visual imagery in people with synesthesia (sensory phenomenon in which one sensory modality automatically triggers sensation in another unrelated modality. For example, some people can taste words that they hear or see colors in black words and shapes.) We found that synesthetes have greater visual imagery usage but not enhanced imagery abilities.

Electrode Visualization Toolbox

In order to interpret the neural activity recorded in each electrode of intracranial EEG, I designed and developed a graphical user interface program using MATLAB and Python to analyze and visualize the neural time series data onto the 3D brain. Additionally, this tool allows automatic labeling of electrode names and generates publication ready images.

Audiovisual Speech Integration

When the brain processes concurrent auditory and visual information, this information is integrated to create a multisensory perception. In this project, we used neural time series analysis from human intracranial recording to investigate when the information from each modality is processed and then integrated. We show that audiovisual processing and integration likely occurs in a parallel, non-serial manner much earlier in the processing hierarchy than is suggested by the popular late integration model.
